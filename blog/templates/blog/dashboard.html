MSTR Investment Thesis

Main investment tracked against Bitcoin purchasing power.

MSTR / BTC

BTC is the core asset. MSTR is the high-beta Bitcoin equity wrapper.

The thesis is not "MSTR goes up in dollars." The thesis is: MSTR should outperform BTC in BTC terms over the next two years because its current BTC-denominated price is depressed, while Strategy continues to accumulate BTC and may regain an mNAV premium if BTC recovers.

Current baseline: MSTR $84.82 / BTC $59,980 = 0.001414 BTC per MSTR share, or about 141,400 sats/share. That is the number to monitor. If it rises, MSTR beats BTC. If it falls, BTC was the better hold.

Strategy currently holds about 847,363 BTC, with total cost around $64.1B and average cost around $75.6k/BTC. Bitcointreasuries shows the stock trading below raw BTC NAV on basic/diluted equity mNAV, but near or above NAV once enterprise value and the capital stack are considered.

Your allocation rule: assuming "2 pieces BTC / 1 piece MSTR" means equal cash chunks, this is 67% BTC / 33% MSTR. BTC remains the fortress, while MSTR is the satellite bet. The risk is not that MSTR fails to rise in USD. The risk is that it rises less than BTC.

Buy BTC as the monetary base layer. Buy MSTR as a capped, high-beta Saylor warrant. Monitor it in sats/share for two years. If MSTR/BTC rises from ~141k sats to 200k-250k sats, the strategy worked. If it stays flat, BTC alone was enough. If it falls below 100k sats, MSTR is consuming risk budget without delivering BTC-denominated alpha.
