Swiss presence and capital rails

Building a calmer base for cross-border wealth.

Hashen supports entrepreneurs, family offices, and German-speaking HNWI families who want a Swiss presence connected to governance, reporting, domicile, and long-term treasury thinking.

International families and entrepreneurs increasingly need more than a bank relationship or a single adviser. They need a calm Swiss base from which ownership, governance, reporting, domicile, and long-term capital decisions can be understood clearly.

Hashen is being built as that coordination layer: a bridge between family, company, adviser network, and the emerging digital economy.

The work starts with clarity of role. Some questions are operational, some are governance questions, and some belong with banks, regulated advisers, lawyers, tax specialists, trustees, or other qualified partners. The value is in making that perimeter clear before complexity builds up.

This is not a product built around trading or asset custody. It is a platform for Swiss presence, family-office coordination, reporting discipline, domicile and governance rails, and future infrastructure that can connect traditional wealth with digital capital.

For family offices, that creates a useful middle layer: high-level enough to stay strategic, practical enough to coordinate real work, and restrained enough to avoid becoming a catch-all fiduciary promise.

Bitcoin remains central to the research view. It is open, scarce, global, and independent settlement infrastructure. Around it, we see opportunities in treasury policy, ownership reporting, cross-border payments, and capital structures better suited to a digital age.

The goal is simple: give families and entrepreneurs a calmer Swiss base from which capital, companies, and responsibilities can move with more clarity.

We exist to provide the tools, research, reporting discipline, and partner coordination needed to act with independence, restraint, and a long time horizon.
